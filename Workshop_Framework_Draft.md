# Vision Stack Workshop Framework

**Target Audience:** UX/Design Leaders & Team Managers (but flexible enough for anyone to facilitate).  
**Format:** Remote, fast-paced (Under 4 hours total).  
**Tools Required:** FigJam (template provided) + LLM of choice (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.).  

---

## What is a Vision Stack?
A Vision Stack is a structured framework that explicitly defines a team's core identity, direction, and operational focus. By layering these five elements—**Principles, Purpose, Mission, Strategy, and OKRs**—a team aligns on *how* they work, *why* they exist, *what* they are doing, *how* they will succeed, and *how* they measure that success. This shared clarity accelerates decision-making and fosters team autonomy.

---

## Workshop Agenda (Total Time: ~3hr 40m + 20m buffer)
*   **0:00 - 0:45**: Principles (45 mins)
*   **0:45 - 1:15**: Purpose (30 mins)
*   **1:15 - 1:55**: Mission (40 mins)
*   **1:55 - 2:15**: Break (20 mins)
*   **2:15 - 3:05**: Strategy (50 mins)
*   **3:05 - 3:45**: OKRs (40 mins)
*   **3:45 - 4:00**: Wrap-up & Next Steps (15 mins)
*(Note: A 20-minute buffer is baked into the timeline for spillover)*

---

## 1. Principles (45 mins)
**Context**: 
Principles are the core values and beliefs that guide behavior, culture, and decision-making within the team. They dictate *how* the team operates on a daily basis.

**Real-world Example**: 
- *Amazon*: "Customer Obsession"
- *Atlassian*: "Don’t #@!% the customer"

**AI-Integrated Activity (High-Velocity):**
*Facilitator Tip: Focus on unique, actionable behaviors rather than generic buzzwords like "Integrity."*

1. **Diverge (7 mins)**: Instruct the team to individually list specific behaviors they value most in their colleagues on FigJam sticky notes. Encourage them to write in full sentences (e.g., "Always assume the user is confused").
2. **AI Synthesis (5 mins)**: 
   - *Facilitator*: Copy all the raw sticky note text from FigJam into your LLM.
   - *Prompt*: `"Act as an expert organizational designer. Review these team behaviors and cluster them into 4-6 distinct thematic principles. For each theme, provide a punchy, memorable title (2-4 words) and a 1-sentence description of the behavior."`
   - Paste the LLM output back onto the FigJam board as text blocks.
3. **Voting & Alignment (15 mins)**: Have the team read the AI-generated themes and use FigJam stamps (+1) to vote on the ones that resonate most. 
4. **Refinement (18 mins)**: Take the top 4-5 voted themes. Discuss as a group and tweak the wording to make them sound authentic to your team's voice.

---

## 2. Purpose (30 mins)
**Context**: 
Purpose is the “why.” It is the fundamental reason a team exists beyond simply doing the work or making money. It should be inspiring and inward-facing, remaining constant even if the product pivots.

**Real-world Example**: 
- *Apple*: "To make a contribution to the world by making tools for the mind that advance humankind."
- *Disney*: "To entertain, inform and inspire people around the globe."

**AI-Integrated Activity (High-Velocity):**
1. **The "Why" Prompts (8 mins)**: Create three areas on the FigJam board: 
   - *Who do we help?*
   - *What is their biggest struggle?*
   - *How does our work change their lives?*
   Have the team drop sticky notes in each area.
2. **AI Generation (5 mins)**: 
   - *Facilitator*: Copy the sticky notes from all three sections into your LLM.
   - *Prompt*: `"Based on these team inputs about who we help, their struggles, and our impact, generate 7 distinct, inspiring 'Purpose Statements'. Each must be exactly one sentence long, evoke emotion, and explain the fundamental reason this team exists."`
   - Paste the options onto the board.
3. **Drafting & Finalizing (17 mins)**: The team quickly reviews and discusses. Use a FigJam timer to force a decision. Combine favorite elements or pick one winning statement explicitly. 

---

## 3. Mission (40 mins)
**Context**: 
Mission is the "what" and "who." It defines the team's current business objective and what they are actively working to achieve over the next 1-3 years. Grounded and clear.

**Real-world Example**: 
- *Google*: "To organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful."
- *Spotify*: "To unlock the potential of human creativity."

**AI-Integrated Activity (High-Velocity):**
1. **Mad-Libs Framework (10 mins)**: Provide the team with a fill-in-the-blank sticky note template in FigJam: 
   *"We build `[solution/product]` for `[specific audience]` so they can `[primary outcome]`."* 
   Everyone fills out 2-3 variations individually.
2. **AI Refinement (5 mins)**: 
   - *Facilitator*: Copy all the completed templates into the LLM.
   - *Prompt*: `"I am a UX/Design leader guiding my team. Analyze these rough mission statement drafts. Synthesize the core ideas into 4 polished, concise mission statements that clearly define what we build, who it's for, and the outcome."`
   - Paste the 4 options back into FigJam.
3. **Debates & Alignment (25 mins)**: This is often the hardest part. Review the 4 options. Have the team debate the specifics. Edit the leading favorite live on the board until there is a general consensus.

*Encourage everyone to step away from their screens.*

## 4. Strategy (50 mins)
**Context**: 
Strategy is the "how." It outlines the specific approach, competitive advantages, and strategic pillars the team will use to achieve the Mission. It’s also about what you choose *not* to do.

**Real-world Example**: 
- *Tesla*: "Build sports car. Use that money to build an affordable car. Use that money to build an even more affordable car."

**AI-Integrated Activity (High-Velocity):**
1. **Strategic Pillars Brainstorm (15 mins)**: Ask the team to identify areas where the team needs focus to win. Have them cluster sticky notes under headers like "User Experience," "Technical Foundation," or "Process." Goal: identify 3-4 major focus areas.
2. **AI Devil's Advocate (10 mins)**: 
   - *Facilitator*: Feed the raw clusters into the LLM.
   - *Prompt*: `"We are establishing our strategic pillars based on these inputs. Act as a critical business strategist. First, summarize these into 3 clear strategic pillars. Then, aggressively critique them: point out what we are failing to prioritize, the risks in this approach, and how to make these pillars more rigorous."`
   - Paste both the summarized pillars and the critique into FigJam.
3. **Iteration & Hard Choices (25 mins)**: Read the LLM's critique aloud. Discuss what the team is willing to sacrifice. Refine the 3 strategic pillars so they are opinionated (e.g., shifting from "Build great UX" to "Prioritize mobile speed over bleeding-edge features").

---

## 5. OKRs (Objectives and Key Results) (40 mins)
**Context**: 
OKRs define how success is measured. Objectives are qualitative and inspiring goals (the destination), while Key Results are quantitative, measurable outcomes (the map).

**Real-world Example**: 
- *Objective*: Delight our mobile users.
- *Key Result 1*: Increase App Store rating from 4.1 to 4.5.
- *Key Result 2*: Reduce app crash rate by 30%.

**AI-Integrated Activity (High-Velocity):**
*Constraint: Maximum 2 Objectives.*
1. **Drafting Objectives (10 mins)**: Looking at the 3 Strategic Pillars just defined, the team collectively agrees on 1-2 top priorities for the upcoming quarter/year. Write them boldly on the board.
2. **AI Metrics Assistant (10 mins)**: 
   - Team brainstorms rough ideas for metrics on stickies (e.g., "less bugs", "faster design handoff"). 
   - *Facilitator Prompt*: `"Here is our Objective: [Insert Objective]. Here are our rough ideas for metrics: [Insert stickies]. Turn these rough ideas into 4 distinct, SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) Key Results. Make them aggressive but realistic."`
3. **Finalization & Reality Check (20 mins)**: Review the AI-generated KRs. The critical question here is: *"Do we actually have the tooling/data to measure this right now?"* If not, adjust the KR until it is measurable today. Select the top 2-3 KRs per Objective.

---

## 6. Wrap-up & Next Steps (15 mins)
**Context:** Don't let the momentum die on the FigJam board. Cement the work.

1. **The Read-Back (5 mins)**: The facilitator reads the completed Vision Stack from top to bottom (Principles -> Purpose -> Mission -> Strategy -> OKRs). Ensure it flows logically and tells a cohesive story.
2. **Commitment (5 mins)**: Ask the team for a verbal/digital thumbs-up commitment to operating under this new stack.
3. **Next Steps (5 mins)**:
   - Assign an owner to move the raw text from FigJam into the team's central documentation hub (Notion, Confluence, etc.) by end of week.
   - **Socialize the Work**: Share the finalized Vision Stack with cross-functional leadership and adjacent teams to establish public accountability and operational transparency.
   - Schedule a 30-minute review session in exactly one month to check progress, specifically against the OKRs.
